MUST VISIT!!...FAMOUS TEMPLES:

ARUNACHALAM

The Arulmigu Arunachaleswarar Temple, also known as Annamalaiyar Temple or Tiruvannamalai Temple, is a significant Hindu temple dedicated to Shiva and Parvathi, located at the base of Arunachala Hill in Tiruvannamalai, Tamil Nadu, India. It's one of the five Pancha Bhoota Sthalas, representing the element of fire (Agni).

KASHI VISHWANATH TEMPLE

The Kashi Vishwanath Temple in Varanasi is special because it's one of the 12 holiest temples dedicated to Lord Shiva (Jyotirlingas), a significant Hindu pilgrimage site, and is believed to be a place where devotees can achieve liberation (moksha).

SRISAILAM:

Srisailam Temple, dedicated to Lord Shiva as Mallikarjuna and his consort Parvati as Bhramaramba, is a significant Hindu pilgrimage site in Andhra Pradesh, revered as one of the 12 Jyotirlingas and one of the 18 Shakti Peethas.

LEPAKSHI

Veerabhadra temple is a Hindu temple located in the Lepakshi, in the state of Andhra Pradesh, India. The temple is dedicated to the Virabhadra, a fierce form of the god Shiva.

SRIKALAHASTI:

The Srikalahasti Temple is located in the town of Srikalahasti, in Tirupati district in the state of Andhra Pradesh, India. The Srikalahasti Temple, dedicated to Lord Shiva, is famous for its Vayu Linga (Wind Lingam), one of the Pancha Bhoota Sthalams representing wind, and its Rahu-Ketu Pooja, believed to alleviate negative astrological effects.

DRAKSHARAMAM

Draksharamam Temple, also known as Draksharama Bheemeshwara Swamy Temple, is a significant Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Shiva, located in the Draksharamam town of Konaseema district in Andhra Pradesh, and is one of the five Pancharama Kshetras.
